About Me

I am a highschooler in Canada who enjoys coding, and tinkering with new things.

Coding Stuff

It all started when I was in grade school. My teacher introduced the class to Scratch — a block-based visual coding website that people from all over the world use to create and share apps and games. When I saw this, I took full advantage of it. I created cool arcade games, story boards, and even went as far as make a calculator that would calculate properties of polygons to help me understand a school assignment.

Fast forward a couple of years, and I moved onto Roblox — a slightly more mature online game platform where people can make and share their creations. This was my first time using any sort of text-based language (this one in particular was derived from Lua, called LuaU). I made many genres of games on this platform, some gaining a couple visits while others gaining hundreds. However, this was a major stepping stone for me, as it introduced me to the basics of a server-client app.

It didn't stop there. Once I entered highschool, I started looking at other languages such as Java and Python. This was arguably one of my best decisions.

The thought of coding in Java or Python was hard to maintain. Since at the time I lacked project ideas, and the enthusiasm towards learning new coding languages slowly died down. After all, as long as I stayed with Roblox and Lua, I could create and publish endless amounts of games. However, I managed to keep a consistent routine, which helped me pull through.

Today, I code on a daily basis. Whether it's for a school project or for fun, a web app or a desktop app, a program that tracks events in a game or a silly stuffy that bounces around at the bottom of the screen, I find a lot of joy in it. I even learned how to use cloud backup software such as GitHub (you can see some stats to the right).

Robotics

In grade 10, I joined my school's robotics team as a software member. This motivated me to pick up Java and Python once again. However this time, I actually had a real-world project where I could apply this knowledge.

I went from being completely clueless, to somewhat knowledgable in the architechture of a robot, to being a key member in the team. The advantage of being in the team is being able to have hands-on experience with real-world machinery, recieve guidance from real professionals, and travel to new places to compete in a fast-paced environment. Even while my primary role was to code a robot, I was able to learn a lot of real-world concepts when it comes to engineering moving parts that I could definetly apply in the future, if I choose to work in a similar environment.

This was a real game changer for me, and it's safe to say that our team achieved a lot.
